Rental Application Policy

How applications work, what the fee covers, and what to expect after you apply.

Effective Date: April 8, 2026

Overview

This policy explains how rental applications work on Choice Properties — what the fee covers, how decisions are made, and what happens after you apply.

Application Fee

Each property listing has an application fee set by the landlord or agent. The fee is displayed on the property listing and again on the application form before you pay. The fee amount varies by property.

The application fee:

  • Is required to submit a complete application
  • Covers the cost of processing, reviewing, and administering your application
  • Is non-refundable once your application is submitted and payment is processed, except as described in the Application Credit Policy

Who Makes the Decision

The landlord or agent managing the property makes all approval decisions. Choice Properties facilitates the process but does not influence or make leasing decisions. Submitting an application does not guarantee approval, reserve the property, or create any tenancy.

What Happens After You Apply

Once your application is submitted:

  1. You receive an email confirmation with your Application ID
  2. The landlord and Choice Properties staff review your application
  3. Typical review time is 24 to 72 business hours
  4. You will receive an email notification when your status changes
  5. You can track your application status at any time using the "Track My Application" link

Application Statuses — What They Mean

If You Are Not Selected

If your application is denied, you will be notified by email. You may receive application credits as described in the Application Credit Policy, which can be applied toward the fee on a future application.

Co-Applicants

One co-applicant may be included on a single application at no additional charge. The co-applicant must meet the same eligibility requirements (age 18+, truthful information). Both applicants agree to the same terms by submitting.

Income Qualification

Applicants are generally expected to demonstrate a gross monthly income of at least three times (3×) the monthly rent. Combined household income (including a co-applicant) may be used. Income from employment, self-employment, government benefits, child support, and other documented sources may be considered. The landlord makes the final determination on income qualification.

Minimum Age

All applicants must be 18 years of age or older. Applications from individuals under 18 will not be accepted.
