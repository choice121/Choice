Rental Application Policy

How applications work, what the fee covers, and what to expect after you apply.

Effective Date: April 8, 2026

Overview

This policy explains how rental applications work on Choice Properties — what the fee covers, how decisions are made, and what happens after you apply.

Process at a Glance

  1. Select a property and submit your application with the required fee.
  2. Review period — our team and the landlord evaluate your application (typically 24–72 business hours).
  3. If approved — you are invited to pay a holding deposit to reserve the property while your lease is prepared.
  4. Lease review — you receive your lease documentation, review it, and sign electronically.
  5. Move-in — the property is fully secured and you receive guidance on remaining move-in steps.

Support is available at every stage. Every step is guided, documented, and designed to be straightforward.

Application Fee

The application fee is a flat $50 per application, covering your background and credit screening.

Nothing is charged when you submit your application. After submission, our leasing team will contact you within 24 hours at the phone number or email provided to securely collect the $50 fee. Your application enters review only after the fee is paid.

The application fee:

  • Is required for your application to be reviewed
  • Covers the cost of processing, reviewing, and administering your application
  • Is non-refundable once payment has been processed, except as described in the Application Credit Policy

Who Makes the Decision

The landlord or agent managing the property makes all approval decisions. Choice Properties facilitates the process but does not influence or make leasing decisions. Submitting an application does not guarantee approval, reserve the property, or create any tenancy.

What Happens After You Apply

Once your application is submitted:

  1. You receive an email confirmation with your Application ID
  2. The landlord and Choice Properties staff review your application
  3. Typical review time is 24 to 72 hours after payment is completed
  4. You will receive an email notification when your status changes
  5. You can track your application status at any time using the "Track Application" link

Background & Credit Checks

As part of the application review process, your application may be subject to a background check and/or credit check, arranged by the landlord or Choice Properties. By submitting an application, you authorize this review.

If a consumer reporting agency is used to evaluate your application, you have the right under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) to:

  • Know the name and contact information of the reporting agency used
  • Receive a free copy of your report from that agency
  • Dispute inaccurate or incomplete information with the reporting agency

These rights exist regardless of the outcome of your application.

If You Are Not Selected

If your application is not approved, you will be notified by email. If the decision was based in whole or in part on information in a consumer report (credit or background check), you will receive an adverse action notice identifying the consumer reporting agency used, so you can obtain a free copy of your report and dispute any inaccuracies.

Additionally, if your application is denied, you are entitled to submit up to two additional applications for other properties without paying an additional application fee. This credit is applied automatically — see the Application Credit Policy for full details and conditions.

Co-Applicants

One co-applicant may be included on a single application at no additional charge. The co-applicant must meet the same eligibility requirements (age 18+, truthful information). Both applicants agree to the same terms by submitting.

Income Qualification

Applicants are generally expected to demonstrate a gross monthly income of at least three times (3×) the monthly rent. Combined household income (including a co-applicant) may be used. Income from employment, self-employment, government benefits, child support, and other documented sources may be considered. The landlord makes the final determination on income qualification.

Minimum Age

All applicants must be 18 years of age or older. Applications from individuals under 18 will not be accepted.
